Traditional Boil

Traditional Crawfish Boil

Single pot and 1 30-40lb sack of crawfish (Serves 8-12)

One way to determine how much water is needed in the pot is to place all of the fruit/vegetables in the bottom of empty pot (outside of basket to keep everything seperated). Put CLEAN crawfish in basket on top of veggies. Fill with water until the top of the water is 2-3 inches above crawfish. Remove everything but the water from the pot and mark the water fill line on a paddle or on the inside of the pot. Marking both is always a good idea.

STEP 1: WASH/PURGE THE CRAWFISH
Dry Ingredients:
4lbs (1 4lb bag) Frog Bone Cajun Seasoning (8 cups)
1 cup cayenne pepper (8oz)
20 bay leaves
1 cup garlic powder (8oz)
2 bags Zatarain's crab boil
1/4 cup Hondashi
Liquid Ingredients:
1 cup Zatarans crab boil (8oz)
1 bottle Louisiana Hot Sauce (6oz)
1 bottle Zatarain's Garlic & Onion (8oz)
1 cup white vinegar
Seasoning Fruits/Vegetables:
8-12 Lemons cut in half *or 32 oz Lemon Juice
8 med-large mandarin oranges cut in half
5 lbs pureed yellow onions
1 head of pureed celery
12 bulbs of Garlic (cut in half)
Meats/Vegetables:
4 Packages of Conecuh sausage
12 Potatoes
24oz (4 packages) baby bella mushrooms
12 frozen precooked corn on the cob (cut in half)
Prep work:
Combine all seasoning fruit/vegetables
Combine all dry ingredients
Combine all pureed ingredients
Combine all liquid ingredients
Cut up sausage
Add water to pot and set burner to HIGH:
Add all liquid seasonings
Add all seasoning fruits/vegetables
Add pureed onions/celery
Place lid on pot
*Approximately 25-30 minutes to start boiling
Once water reaches a rolling boil:
Add all dry ingredients
Place lid on pot
Boil for 10 minutes with lid on:
Add potatoes
Reduce heat enough to maintain a rolling boil
Place lid on pot
Boil for another 10 minutes:
Add Conecuh sausage
Place lid on pot
Boil for 5 minutes:
Set burner to HIGH
Add crawfish
Place lid on pot
Approximately 10 minutes to reach a rolling boil
Once the pot has returned to a rolling boil:
Add mushrooms
Let the crawfish continue to cook for another 2 minutes
After the crawfish have cooked in a rolling boil for 2 minutes (should be floating):
Turn off propane
Add FROZEN corn
Try to push the floating vegetables below the crawfish
Add a 5lb bag of ice to stop the cooking process
Do not put the lid back on after this point
The ideal temperature range to soak the crawfish is 150-160 degrees
Soak for 20-30 minutes (taste test at 15)

Steaming

Steamed Crawfish

The Unconventional Approach

Multiple pots are ideal but not necessary for steaming crawfish. A large cooler can be used as a continer for the soak. Just make sure that you have a large strainer to remove the crawfish after they're done soaking. The ingredients below are good for up to 200lbs of crawfish. You will definitely want to puree your onions and celery. Also needed is a thermometer.

STEP 1: WASH/PURGE THE CRAWFISH
Dry Ingredients:
6lbs (4lb bag + 2lb bag) Frog Bone Cajun Seasoning (12 cups)
2 cups cayenne pepper (16oz)
20 bay leaves
2 cups garlic powder (8oz)
2 bags Zatarain's crab boil
1/2 cup Hondashi
Liquid Ingredients:
3 cups Zatarans crab boil (8oz)
1 bottle Louisiana Hot Sauce (6oz)
2 bottles Zatarain's Garlic & Onion (8oz)
1 cup white vinegar
Seasoning Fruits/Vegetables:
32 oz Lemon Juice
16 oz Orange Juice
5 lbs pureed yellow onions
1 head of pureed celery
Meats/Vegetables:
4 Packages of Conecuh sausage
12 Potatoes
24oz (4 packages) baby bella mushrooms
12 frozen precooked corn on the cob (cut in half)
Prep work:
Combine all seasoning fruit/vegetables
Combine all dry ingredients
Combine all pureed ingredients
Combine all liquid ingredients
Cut up sausage
Add water to pot and set burner to HIGH:
Add all liquid seasonings
Add all seasoning fruits/vegetables
Add pureed onionis/celery
Place lid on pot
*Approximately 25-30 minutes to start boiling
Once water reaches a rolling boil:
Add all dry ingredients
Place lid on pot
Boil for 10 minutes with lid on:
Add potatoes
Reduce heat enough to maintain a rolling boil
Place lid on pot
Boil for another 10 minutes:
Add Conecuh sausage
Add mushrooms
Place lid on pot
Boil for 5 minutes:
Turn off propane
Add FROZEN corn
Spray the outside of the pot with cold water the until the soak reaches a temperature of 165 degrees
Once the soak reaches a temperature of 165 degrees:
Stop spraying the outside of the pot
Let the meats/veggies soak for 20-30 miutes to taste
When the soaking process is complete:
Dump the meats/veggies in a cooler to keep warm
Remove the pot from the burner-this will now be the soaking pot
Put approximately 2 inches of water in the second pot-this will be the steaming pot
Turn burner on HIGH until the water reaches a rolling boil
Once a rolling boil is acheived:
Place the crawfish in the pot
Put lid on pot
Leave burner on HIGH
Once a foam covers all of the crawfish:
Let the crawfish continue to steam for 4-1/2 minutes
After 4-1/2 minutes have passed:
Shut off burner
Move the crawfish to the soaker pot
Soak for 20-30 minutes (taste test at 15)
Repeat the steaming process as needed for up to 200lbs of crawfish

Lessons

Lessons Learned

Lessons Learned the Hard Way

Here are a few things that I've learned over the years about boiling crawfish.

Approximately 5-10% of your crawfish will be DOA. This is normal when they have to be transported long distances.
Don't submerge them in water that isn't circulating for longer than a few minutes. Though crawfish won't drown in water, they will suffocate (drowm) from a lack of oxygen.
They will die if their gills get too dry. Its best to cover them with ice and an extremely wet towell when they're not in water.
Salt isn't necessary for purging. Fresh water from the tap or hose will do the trick.
Have a minimum of 2 propane tanks available for every crawfish boil.
Don't dispose of crawfish heads indoors. After everyone is done eating, bag up and dispose of them as soon as possible. Don't forget about the DOA crawfish that were removed during the washing/purging process. Those suckers smell BAD; especially after they've sat around for a day or two.
The soak that gives the crawfish they're incredible flavor can kill your grass if you empty your pot while the water is still hot.
